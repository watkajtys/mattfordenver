Matt Watkajtys for Denver Mayor

Who? Matt Watkajtys

Matt has rented in the Golden Triangle in Denver since 2017. He worked as a product development consultant before covid. When Covid hit, Matt's income fell off a cliff. He applied for unemployment. He is currently utilizing the Emergency Rent Relief program in 2021. He commutes mostly by ebike and trains his dog in detection work!

Political experience?

No. Startup experiences. Looking at metrics, finding consensus, shipping results. The goal would be to hire the best domain experts we can find for critical city leadership. Denver needs visionary leadership. The city is running out of runway to make changes.

The Revitilization

Denver is rebuilding after Covid. Things will never be the same. That's a fact. The cities that thrive will be the ones to notice the world has changed.

  • Last Mile Infrastructure and a failed vision zero
    • Sweeping and immediate infrastructure adjustments
    • A prioritization of the 5280 loop
    • An immediate deployment of protected bikeways/scooterways throughout the city.
    • Traffic easing measures on eligible and problematic streets
    • A reclaimation of eligible streets to pedestrian and car-free areas.
    • A citywide initiative to rebuild (or build) our sidewalks.
  • A Comprehensive Approach to Housing Stability
    • Immediate "low-hanging fruit" updates at shelter facilities.
    • Immediate expansion of safe-camping centers.
    • Expansion of eviction defense and emergency rent relief programs.
    • Year over Year rent stabilization capped at prime + tbd
    • Supporting safe parking initiatives
  • Public Safety Reformations
    • Crackdown on Catalytic Converter and Car Thefts
    • Expanding foot patrols across all neighborhoods.
    • Decrypting general emergency dispatch in Denver
    • A concerted cleanup effort in highly trafficked public areas
  • Fentanyl - a radical approach is required
    • Expansion of safe injection sites throughout Denver
    • Free essential supplies in free vending machines on city property containing testing materials, first aid, sexual health, and drug health.
    • More programming
  • Scooters
    • Infrastructure first. Immediate deployments of bikeways/scooterways on already traveled routes.
    • Removing the vendor cap - we need more last mile solutions, not fewer. I miss denver BCycle. I miss cheap scooter and ebike rides.
    • Expansion of scooter/bike parking areas on the bikeways/scooterways
  • Vision Zero
    • Has failed.
    • Infrastucture first. Traffic easing measures, dedicated non-car lanes on eligible roads.
    • Light retiming across the Denver Metro
  • A Changing Downtown
    • Incentivize conversions from office space to mixed use residential space.
  • Unleashed Dogs
    • Dedicated "barky hour" at public parks with the most offleash occurences in the mornings and evenings. Zero tolerance policy in parks outside of those hours.
  • A changing climate and Denver
    • Expansion of Denver's centralized steam system. Hook up as many buildings to the system as possible.
    • A wide deployment of trees for treelawns throughout the city. Trees reduce ambient temperatures.
    • An audit of climate vulnerabilities specific to Denver - from fire threats to flash flooding events, Denver needs a plan.
  • Emergency Preparedness
    • Citywide plans for every national security emergency possible.
    • Particular attention paid to excessive heat, rain, and localized fire threats
    • Prepare additional cooling centers, make more water refill stations available citywide
    • Extensive crowd-control training by all necessary officials
