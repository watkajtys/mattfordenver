Matt Watkajtys for Denver Mayor

Who? Matt Watkajtys (Wa-Kite-Us)

Matt has rented in the Golden Triangle in Denver since 2017. He worked as a product development consultant before covid. When Covid hit, Matt's income fell off a cliff. He applied for unemployment. He is currently utilizing the Emergency Rent Relief program in 2021. He commutes mostly by ebike and trains his dog in detection work!

Political experience?

No. Startup experience. Looking at metrics, finding consensus, shipping results. The goal would be to hire the best domain experts we can find for critical city leadership. We hire the best. We streamline. We act.
Denver needs a vision. I have one.

Why Run?

This is the single most import election cycle in Denver's recent history. We haven't yet seen the politicians enter the mayoral race. I'm worried.
That's why I'm running. The next few years will have to run closer to a startup. Denver will need to move quicker than it's ever moved before.

Hard Conversations to Have

Denver. We have to talk. Our city is having problems. It's not just us. It's nationwide.

It's time to admit we have problems. Our downtown is threatened. Our communities are threatened. So we need to talk. Let me tell you what I'm about.

The moment the world locked down in 2020, everything changed. Denver needs to change as quickly as Covid has impacted us.

It'll hurt. We need to admit our roads have gotten away from us. We'll have to lose a few parking spaces and shift some roads to one-ways. You'll have to suffer through light re-timings and new traffic patterns. We'll have to take radical action on homelessness and fentanyl. Some of these changes will be scary and sound counter-intuitive. But they are steps we will have to take.

    • Bikes don't follow the rules! They ran a stop sign!
      Behold. The Idaho stop. It's now state law. A stop sign becomes a yield. I commute by bike, I'll explain. 1) Stop sign approaches. It's clear ahead of me. I have a wider field of view than a car. I look left, right. That's clear. I throttle back up and go through. A yield. 2) Stop light approaches. I try to filter up towards the front of the line if possible. Then I stop. I'm at a stop sign. If things clear up and I get a space, I start going. Why do we do this? 1) I see you. I promise you. I don't want to die. 2) I can see more than you. 3) Starting back up is incredibly labor-intensive - it's more efficient. 4) At a light, I can filter up and be at speed by the time you catch up. Safer for everyone! Yes, people bike poorly. People also drive poorly. You've seen as many red lights ran as I have.
